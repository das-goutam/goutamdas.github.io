About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the School of Aeronautics and Astronautics at Purdue University, working under the supervision of Professor Takashi Tanaka. My current research focuses on cyber-physical security and deception in autonomous systems using Monte Carlo methods, funded by DARPA and AFOSR.

I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from George Mason University in 2025, where I worked in the Collaborative Robotics, Autonomy and Dynamics Lab (CRADL) under the supervision of Professor Daigo Shishika. My dissertation, titled "Cooperative Team Behavior via Role Specialization in Adversarial Multi-Agent Games," developed novel strategies for multi-agent coordination in adversarial settings.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from Chittagong University of Engineering and Technology (CUET), Bangladesh, and worked as a Product Design Engineer at Walton Hi-Tech Industries.

Research Projects

Project Image
2025–2026

Dynamic Assignment Switching and Singular Surfaces

Developed closed-form characterization of Assignment Switching Singular Surface (AS3) and classified switching dynamics using Filippov framework for multi-agent pursuit-evasion scenarios. Proved that dynamic assignment switching breaks open-loop equilibrium and established computable performance bounds through Stackelberg evader strategy.

Game Theory Differential Games Multi-Agent Systems

Under review: IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control

Project Image
2026

Hybrid Game-Theoretic and RL Framework for Border Defense

Developed a hybrid approach combining multi-agent reinforcement learning with game-theoretic baseline policies for multi-agent border defense scenarios, achieving faster convergence than standalone methods. Implemented early termination via differential game solutions upon attacker detection, reducing computational overhead.
